EcoMon Cruise

My first research cruise I went out on the NOAA EcoMon survey for 18 days in 2021. The mission was to collect zooplankton and ichthyoplankton across the north Atlantic shelf. The stations are randomly distributed across many strata that are defined based on the bathymetry of the shelf, and at every station bongo nets are deployed, and CTD information is collected across a depth profile. At select stations, the CTD rosette is also deployed to collect water samples as well.

These data are important because it monitors the ecosystem response at a seasonal level over the course of many years. The survey has been in operation since the 70's in different capacities, and because this data was available, I was able to ask questions about long term change in the Gulf of Main food webs as part of my PhD thesis. Many people had to come before me to design research programs, make observations, and to archive and document the data to make the research I have done and contributed to possible. Having the opportunity to participate in the survey was important to me not only because I got to understand the data I was working on better, but also because I wanted to contribute to the collection of data for other scientists to use in the future as well.
