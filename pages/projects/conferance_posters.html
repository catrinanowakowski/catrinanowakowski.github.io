Conferance Poster Presentations

In my second year of graduate school, I transitioned in to a food web ecology lab where I started working with Zooplankton and deep sea coral! This is the poster I presented at my first ocean sciences (OSM 2020) in my third year of grad school just before the covid lock downs.

I spent my first year of graduate school developing numerical models of hurricane boundary layers, and while the lab was not the best fit for me to finish a PhD in, it was a transformative time for me learning how to develop software, version control between multiple people, and work in HPC environments. I revamped two hurricane models to include higher resolution calculations, surface roughness interactions, and many other features, all working in Fortran. This poster highlights a portion of the work I did during this first year.

Working with Marina Astitha at the University of Connecticut on Hazardous Agal Blooms in Lake Erie set my career on a track I didn't even know how to picture when I first started. It involved a lot of first for me, from airport travel to applying statistics. I even got to work with folks at the US EPA's Office of Research and Development, where I now hold a postdoc position! I attended my first conference (CMAQ in The Research Triangle area, NC) and won the best student poster!
