In my second year of graduate school, I transitioned in to a food web ecology lab where I started working with zooplankton and deep sea coral! This is the poster I presented at my first ocean sciences (OSM 2020) in my third year of grad school just before the covid lockdowns.

I spent my first year of graduate school developing numerical models of hurricane boundary layers, and while the lab was not the best fit for me to finish a PhD in, it was a transformitive time for me learning how to develop software, version control between multiple people, and work in HPC environments. I revamped two hurricane models to include higher resolution calculations, surface roughness interactions, and many other features, all working in Fortran. This poster hilights a portion of the work I did during this frist year.
